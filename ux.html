UX School

Overview

In 2020, I attended a workshop on UI/UX design. In the workshop I learned how to conduct UX research and to test out my skills I came up with a concept and started working on it. The concept that I came up with was, UX School, an educational agency helping beginners and teaching them the basics. I did competitive analysis and logo design for the agency.

Role

UX research / Competitive analysis / Logo design

Timeline

January 2021

Outlining Web-app features

Learning Paths

Dedicated learning paths for every profession, UI designer, Product Designer, UX Unicorn, etc.

Difficulties divided into 3 parts

The courses will be divided into 3 parts - beginner, intermediate and advanced. To proceed to higher difficulty the user will need to give an assessment test

Standalone courses

Not all courses will be part of learning paths, some will be available as standalone course like - xD essentials or Introduction to Figma, etc.

Competitive Analysis

This step required me to some market research on the UI/UX learning websites, courses and materials available on the web. I considered this an important part of the step as they can help me understand how they approach the user and help me identifying current trends in the industry and drop some rare gems that can help me in my quest.

Why

It is one the most largest and most acknowledge MOOC's website in the world. Also it's most popular amongst the students, who are our main target audience.

What is:

Coursera is an online platform which gives out many online MOOC's courses, masterclasses etc.

What if:

As it provides many courses, we will just focus on UX courses. The UX courses that are available on this platform are somewhat out-dated. Upgrading the materials can help the ratings of the course and the number of people attending/taking the course.

What works

The prospect of free courses available is what tempts a person. You can even audit the course and then decide if you want a certification or not (after audit you can get the certification for $60). You can choose between course, online degree or specializations.

What bugs

The quizzes and assignments are mostly machine graded. The courses are academic and less industry relevant

Why

What is:

It is a non-profit organization with the goal to raise the level of global design education, while at the same time reduce costs to as low as possible.

What if:

What works

The courses provided by IDF are industry recognized and are taught by leading industry experts and top academics, and the courses are graded by real-life humans too. The 30 day money back guarantee doesn't hurt. Also has a very supportive community worldwide. It solely focuses on UI/UX design and design thinking, the membership grants access to all courses.

What bugs

The problems that I perceived are, you can only buy annual plans, as no monthly plans are available, also there's no option to sign for single courses.

What I learned?

Money back guarantee

As most beginners are just getting a feel for design thinking and UI/UX, it's important to give them an assurance that if they think the field is not for them, they can choose to opt out any time they like.

Teaching and Grading

The teaching process should be a mix of 3 main things - 1.Video materials 2.Reading Material 3.Hands-on approach. Also the various assignments should be graded by real-life humans instead of machines.

Pricing Plans

I felt that we should also provide a monthly plans along with the annual plans. As there can arise a situation where a user will not be free for the month and would therefore feel that his money was not utilized.

Accreditation

After the course completion the certifications provided should be accredited by a reliable third party, as it would add significant value and would be accepted in the industry.
